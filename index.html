Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know which bed setup is best for me?

It all comes down to personal preference! If you prefer a more modern look, we have bed frame options that do not require a foundation or box spring. These modern upholstered beds can dramatically enhance your wall decor and overall room aesthetic.

For a more traditional bed setup, the Casper foundation pairs with our metal bed frame for extra height and seamless support. Our foundation can also be used with any standard-size bed frame that requires a box spring to support the mattress.

Casper bed frames are made from high quality materials like our solid wood beds or metal frames and can complement any bedroom furniture setup.

What is the best bed frame to buy?

The best bed frame depends on your personal style, bedroom space, and budget. Consider factors like material (wood, metal, or upholstered), height preferences, storage needs, and overall aesthetic.

Our platform beds are excellent for modern minimalist spaces, while traditional frames work well with classic decor. If you need extra storage, consider a bed frame with built-in drawers or a higher profile that allows for under-bed storage.

What are the different types of bed frames?

There are several types of bed frames to choose from:

Platform beds: Feature a solid or slatted base that supports the mattress without needing a box spring.

Traditional frames: Require a box spring or foundation for mattress support.

Adjustable bases: Allow you to customize your sleeping position electronically.

Storage beds: Include built-in drawers or lift-up storage compartments.

Metal frames: Durable and often more affordable options with a minimalist design.

How much do bed frames usually cost?

Bed frame prices vary widely depending on material, size, and features. Basic metal frames can start around $100-$200, while high-quality wooden or upholstered frames typically range from $300-$1,500.

Premium options with advanced features like adjustable bases or luxury materials can cost $2,000 or more. Consider your budget alongside durability and warranty when making your decision.

What is the best material for a bed frame?

The best material depends on your priorities:

Solid wood: Offers durability, timeless appeal, and excellent stability. Great for long-term investment.

Metal: Provides modern aesthetics, easy maintenance, and strong support at an affordable price point.

Upholstered: Delivers comfort, style, and a softer look that complements contemporary decor.

Consider factors like your bedroom style, maintenance preferences, and how long you plan to keep the frame when choosing your material.
